Business Analysis Framework

PESTLE Analysis

Key external factors affecting UK businesses — applicable across all sectors.

P
Political
Government & policy
  • Post-Brexit trade rules
    New customs checks and import/export costs push up prices and complicate supply chains.
  • Government spending cuts
    Austerity policies reduce public sector contracts and consumer confidence.
  • National Living Wage rises
    Government-mandated annual increases directly raise labour costs for all employers.
  • Corporation tax at 25%
    Raised in 2023, reducing post-tax profits for businesses of all sizes.
E
Economic
Macro & market conditions
  • High UK inflation (CPI)
    Drove up costs of materials, energy and wages significantly since 2022.
  • Bank of England interest rates
    Higher rates increase borrowing costs, discouraging investment and expansion.
  • Cost of living crisis
    Squeezed consumer disposable income reduces demand for non-essential goods and services.
  • Weak pound sterling
    Makes imports more expensive, raising costs for businesses sourcing from abroad.
S
Social
Demographics & culture
  • Ageing UK population
    Over-65s are the fastest-growing demographic, shifting demand and shrinking the workforce.
  • Rise of ethical consumerism
    UK consumers increasingly choose sustainable or ethically sourced brands.
  • Remote/hybrid work culture
    Reduced demand for office space and commuter services; changed employee expectations.
  • Mental health awareness
    Growing pressure on employers to invest in staff wellbeing, increasing HR costs.
T
Technological
Innovation & disruption
  • AI & automation
    Cutting labour costs and improving efficiency, but threatening job displacement across sectors.
  • Growth of e-commerce
    UK is one of Europe's largest online markets — businesses must adapt or lose share.
  • Cybersecurity threats
    Rising data breaches expose businesses to UK GDPR fines and reputational damage.
  • Cloud computing
    Lowers IT costs but creates dependency on third-party providers and connectivity.
L
Legal
Compliance & legislation
  • UK GDPR
    Non-compliance risks fines up to £17.5m or 4% of annual global turnover.
  • Employment law
    Unfair dismissal, redundancy pay and workers' rights are tightly regulated and enforced.
  • Health & Safety at Work Act 1974
    Legal duty of care to employees; breaches can result in prosecution and unlimited fines.
  • Consumer Rights Act 2015
    Goods and services must be fit for purpose — creates liability for all UK businesses.
E
Environmental
Sustainability & climate
  • UK Net Zero 2050 target
    Government pressure to cut emissions affects operations and reporting obligations.
  • Energy costs
    UK energy prices spiked post-2022; remain a major overhead for most businesses.
  • Plastic Packaging Tax
    £210.83 per tonne on packaging with under 30% recycled content, raising production costs.
  • Extreme weather risk
    Flooding and heatwaves increasingly disrupt UK supply chains, premises and logistics.

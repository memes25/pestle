Business Analysis Framework

SWOT Analysis

Generic internal and external factors for UK businesses — applicable across all sectors.

S
Strengths
Internal positives
  • Established brand reputation
    UK consumers trust consistent brands — drives repeat custom, loyalty and word-of-mouth without extra spend.
  • Skilled, diverse workforce
    The UK labour market is well-educated; businesses can draw on a wide range of specialisms across sectors.
  • Strong digital infrastructure
    High broadband coverage and smartphone penetration let UK businesses operate and sell online efficiently.
  • Customer loyalty base
    Repeat customers reduce acquisition costs and improve long-term revenue stability.
  • Access to UK funding
    British Business Bank loans, government grants and investor funding provide capital for growth and expansion.
W
Weaknesses
Internal negatives
  • High operating costs
    National Living Wage, business rates and energy bills make UK cost bases significantly higher than many international competitors.
  • Supplier dependency
    Over-reliance on single suppliers — particularly post-Brexit — creates vulnerability to price hikes and disruption.
  • Limited marketing budget
    Smaller UK businesses often lack resources to compete with larger rivals on advertising reach and brand awareness.
  • Skills gaps
    Post-Brexit reduction in EU labour supply has created shortages in hospitality, logistics, construction and social care.
  • Slow technology adoption
    Many UK SMEs lag behind on automation and digital tools, reducing efficiency and competitiveness over time.
O
Opportunities
External positives
  • Growing e-commerce market
    The UK is one of Europe's biggest online retail markets — investing in digital channels can significantly grow reach.
  • Green economy growth
    UK Net Zero commitments are driving demand for sustainable products — early movers gain competitive advantage.
  • New trade agreements
    Post-Brexit UK is pursuing independent deals (e.g. CPTPP) that could open new export markets for UK businesses.
  • AI and automation tools
    Affordable AI tools let smaller businesses automate tasks, cut costs and compete more effectively with larger rivals.
  • Government business support
    Grants, R&D tax credits and Innovate UK funding offer real financial opportunities, especially for startups and SMEs.
T
Threats
External negatives
  • Recession risk
    Slow UK GDP growth and high household debt could tip the economy into recession, cutting consumer spending sharply.
  • Intense competition
    Multinationals and low-cost online rivals (Amazon, Shein, Temu) can undercut UK businesses on price and delivery speed.
  • Regulatory changes
    Post-Brexit UK law diverging from EU standards — businesses trading with Europe face rising compliance complexity and cost.
  • Cybersecurity threats
    Rising cyberattacks risk data breaches, operational disruption and heavy UK GDPR fines for businesses of all sizes.
  • Shifting consumer behaviour
    Rapidly changing preferences around sustainability, value and digital mean businesses that don't adapt quickly lose share.

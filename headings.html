Marketing Campaign Exam

Exam Headings

Full reference guide for every heading, plus a condensed A4 cheat sheet.

1
Background
⏱ 5 mins▼
  • Who is the campaign for? — Name the business and describe what they do in one sentence.
  • Why is it being conducted? — State the business problem or opportunity (e.g. falling sales, new product, new market).
  • What does it hope to achieve? — Brief overview of the intended outcome (awareness, sales growth, image).
  • State your Part B decision clearly — e.g. "I am following Decision A because..." Give a specific reason linked to case study data.
2
Aims and Objectives
⏱ 15 mins▼
  • Aim — Write the main aim(s) directly from the case study. Broad and directional.
  • Objective 1 (SMART) — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. E.g. "To increase online sales by 15% within 6 months."
  • Justify Obj 1 — Why is it realistic? Link to case study data. How would it be achieved?
  • Objective 2 (SMART) — A second distinct objective with time frame and measurable target.
  • Justify Obj 2 — Evidence + method of achievement.
3
Research
⏱ 20 mins▼
Primary Research
  • Main findings — One solid paragraph. Use specific figures where given (e.g. "72% of respondents said...").
  • Campaign impact — How do findings shape decisions about target market, message or channel?
  • Evaluate it — Sample size (too small?), who was asked (right demographic?), where (appropriate?), how could it be improved?
Secondary — Trends & Market Size
  • Explain what the data shows. Growing or shrinking? Market size/value? Link to campaign opportunity.
Secondary — Competition Table
  • Table comparing at least 2 competitors across the 7Ps. What to copy or differentiate? How competitive? Growing? New entrants?
Validity
  • How many sources? Trustworthy (official, academic, industry)? Recent (within 2–3 years)?
4
SWOT
⏱ 15 mins▼
  • Strengths — One paragraph. What does the business do well? Link to how it helps the campaign.
  • Weaknesses — One paragraph. Internal limitations and how they hinder the campaign.
  • Opportunities — One paragraph. External positives and how the campaign can exploit them.
  • Threats — One paragraph. External risks and what needs planning for.
5
PESTLE
⏱ 15 mins▼
  • One short paragraph per letter (P / E / S / T / L / E).
  • Describe the factor, then always end: "this means that..." linking it to the campaign impact.
  • Use the PESTLE page on this site for specific UK factors to draw from.
6
Summary
⏱ 5 mins▼
  • Pull out 3–5 key points from research, SWOT and PESTLE that will directly help or hinder the campaign.
  • Keep it brief — a signpost into the plan, not a repeat of everything above.
7
Marketing Message
⏱ 2 mins▼
  • What is the business telling the customer? State the core message — what should customers think, feel or do?
  • 1–2 sentences. Simple, memorable, consistent with the USP.
  • E.g. "We offer affordable, sustainable products that never compromise on quality."
8
Target Market
⏱ 5 mins▼
  • Demographic — Age, gender, income, occupation, family status.
  • Geographic — Location, urban/rural, regional focus.
  • Psychographic — Lifestyle, values, interests, personality.
  • Behavioural — Buying habits, brand loyalty, usage rate, occasion.
9
Marketing Mix — 7Ps
⏱ 45 mins▼
Product
  • Range, quality, ingredients/contents, packaging, USP — what makes it unique vs competitors?
Price
  • Choose a strategy: Skimming (high, niche/luxury), Penetration (low to gain share), Competition-based (match rivals), Discriminatory (different prices for groups), Psychological (£9.99), Cost-plus (cost + margin). Link to competitor pricing and target market income.
Place
  • Where sold? Physical, online, third-party, app? Distribution: direct, wholesale, franchise, digital?
Process
  • Order journey, delivery, payment methods (card, PayPal, BNPL), after-sales, returns, email sign-up, account registration.
Physical Evidence
  • Website — content, ease of use, mobile-friendly. In-store: layout, signage, branding consistency.
People
  • Staff type, how used, training, uniforms, tone of communication.
Promotion
  • Methods from Part B decision — justify each one. How does each carry the marketing message? Link every choice to the target market.
10
Budget
⏱ 15 mins▼
  • List each media form with cost breakdown. Use the case study budget — do not exceed it.
  • For each: how used, why chosen, cost, expected return.
  • Table format: Media | How Used | Cost | Justification.
11
Timescale
⏱ 10 mins▼
  • Something must run every single month — no gaps in the campaign period.
  • For each month: activity, channel, how it reaches the target market.
  • Link each month back to the overall aims and SMART objectives.
  • Table format: Month | Activity | Channel | How it reaches target market.
12
Conclusion
⏱ 5 mins▼
  • Why will it succeed? — Refer to SMART objectives, research findings and the marketing mix.
  • How measured? — Sales figures, social metrics, website traffic, customer surveys.
  • When? — Mid-campaign review (e.g. 3 months) + full review at campaign end.
